    • Message

    DIRECTOR GENERAL & INSPECTOR GENERAL OF POLICE, KARNATAKA STATE

         It indeed is a great honour and privilege for me to lead one lakh strong state police force for serving 6.5 crore people of Karnataka. Karnataka State Police has been pioneer in leveraging technology at least a decade before rest of the country. Be it CCTNS,Cyber Crime Police Station, in house state of art Police Data Centre or Automated Fingerprints Identification System (AFIS); we have been the leaders.On the welfare front, Arogya Bhagya for the health of policemen and their families has been replicated by many other states. We are the pioneers in starting absolutely transparent and objective system for the recruitment of constabulary and Sub-Inspectors. The next step is to integrate CCTNS with the courts and prisons under the aegis of ICJS.

         In addition to maintenance of law & order, communal harmony and prevention of crime, our top priority is to take above technological initiatives to the next level, with focus on cyber crime, crime against women and children and menace of drugs.

         Soon we would launch Seva Sindhu i.e., delivering 18 services including all types of police verifications electronically without any leg work or physical contact with police, whether at the application stage or at the stage of receiving the certificate.

         Strengthening of Forensic Science Laboratory with special focus on Cyber Forensic, DNA testing and Finger Print Bureau would be done on priority.

         We need to ensure prompt response to citizens in distress by ensuring Emergency Response System, commonly known as Dial-112. Bengaluru City has already tasted the fruits of Namma-100 where every distress call is picked up in 15 seconds and physically attended   in 15 minutes. A similar facility will be extended to rural areas. Dial-112 supported by Hoysalas all over the State will become a reality in a phased manner.

         Citizen can’t be served well unless our own constabulary is well looked after. We will improve working conditions of constabulary by providing them good training environment, i.e., training schools, good work environment i.e., police stations and good staying environment i.e. houses. Police Gruha-2020 which has added 11000 quarters will get conclude in the current year. Police Gruh-2025will take off in current year to create at least 60% satisfaction level of housing.

         Simultaneously we will ensure better career prospects by ensuring that every policeman and officer get at least 2 promotions and preferably 3 promotions in his career. No policemen would be made to wait for his promotion or legitimate dues from Government. Yearly health check-up and psychological well being shall be insisted upon.

         However,no police force can deliver without active involvement of citizens. With your support and constructive criticism, we will leave no stone unturned to make the state safe and secure place for everyone.

           Look forward to your valuable cooperation in all our endeavours.

                                 sd/-

                       ( Praveen Sood, IPS )

                       Director General &

                 Inspector General of Police,

                        Karnataka State.

    About Us

    Government of Karnataka (GoK) is a pioneer in leveraging information and communication technology (ICT) for better governance and is at the forefront of implementation of electronic-Governance (e-Governance) initiatives in the country. The Centre for e-Governance (CeG) is a nodal agency established in the year 2006, under the Societies Registration Act, to steer the e-governance policies and strategies in the state. It is the custodian of the e-governance core infrastructure and basic administrative reform based applications created in the state. In fact, it is unique in the state as it is placed in Department of Personnel & Administrative Reforms (DPAR) which is directly accountable to the Chief Minister of the state. The services of CeG over the years has proved to be immensely contributive in realizing the benefits of IT for common man. True to the ethos of the IT sector that demands autonomous functionality, the CeG spreads open its wings to various innovative projects aiming to empower citizens through the support of e-Governance.


    Today, the state can boast of state-of-the-art two Data Centers, MPLS technology based Karnataka State Wide Area Network and core applications such as e-procurement and HRMS groomed under CeG with cent percent performance quality and standards, even setting certain benchmarks.
